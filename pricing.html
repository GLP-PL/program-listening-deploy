Frequently asked questions

What does implementation involve?

Implementation is a structured setup phase where we configure Program Listening to your delivery environment. This includes understanding how delivery, governance, and escalation actually work; defining the signals that matter in your context; configuring coverage, cadence, and reporting; and validating early insight before live monitoring begins. Implementation typically takes 2–4 weeks, depending on complexity.

Is implementation included in the monthly price?

No. Implementation is scoped and priced separately before any subscription begins. This ensures Program Listening is correctly configured to your environment and avoids generic or misaligned monitoring. Monthly pricing only starts once implementation is complete and agreed.

Why is implementation required?

Program Listening is not off-the-shelf software. Every organisation experiences delivery strain differently. Implementation ensures we are listening for the right signals, in the right places, at the right cadence. Skipping this step would reduce accuracy and value.

What happens if Program Listening isn't a fit?

That's exactly what the implementation alignment call is designed to determine. If we don't believe Program Listening will deliver meaningful value in your environment, we'll say so before any commitment is made. There is no obligation to proceed beyond the discovery call.

Can we change plans later?

Yes. Plans can be adjusted as your delivery environment changes. Upgrades take effect immediately, and downgrades apply at the next billing cycle. Most organisations start with a focused scope and expand coverage over time.

Are there any surprise fees?

No. All costs are discussed and agreed upfront during implementation scoping. There are no hidden charges or unexpected add-ons.

Program Listening is designed to earn its place through clarity and early insight – not long-term lock-in.
