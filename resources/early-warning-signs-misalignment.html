Early warning signs your business is misaligned

Misalignment rarely announces itself. Learn to recognise the subtle signals that indicate your organisation is pulling in different directions.

8 min readLeadership

The hidden cost of misalignment

Organisational misalignment is one of the most expensive problems a business can face - yet it rarely appears on any risk register. Teams working at cross-purposes, priorities that conflict without anyone realising, decisions that get made in silos and undermine each other.

The damage compounds silently. By the time misalignment becomes visible - in missed targets, customer complaints, or departing talent - significant value has already been destroyed.

Recognising the early signals

Misalignment shows up in patterns before it shows up in results. Here are the signals to watch for:

Decisions that keep getting revisited

When the same decisions come up repeatedly in different forums, it often signals underlying disagreement about direction that has not been resolved.

Meetings that end without clarity

Teams leave meetings with different understandings of what was agreed. Actions get interpreted differently by different groups.

Competing narratives

Different parts of the organisation tell different stories about priorities, progress, and problems. There is no shared view of reality.

Escalation as default

Issues that should be resolved between teams consistently get pushed up to leadership. This suggests teams lack a shared framework for resolving conflicts.

Heroic effort required for routine work

When normal operations require exceptional effort, it often means the system is working against itself - processes designed without coordination.

Why misalignment persists

Several factors allow misalignment to grow unchecked:

  • Everyone is too busy executing to step back and notice the pattern
  • Raising alignment issues feels like criticism of colleagues
  • There is no safe forum for surfacing cross-team tensions
  • Leadership assumes alignment exists because it was stated once

From symptoms to solutions

Addressing misalignment starts with visibility. Leaders need to see the patterns that indicate teams are pulling in different directions - before those patterns produce visible damage.
